Conference Keynote

The highlight of NYC Camp was undoubtedly the Saturday afternoon keynote address by UN CITO Atti Riazi. Speaking in the mini-general assembly room to as many Drupalists as attended Boston Drupalcon in 2008, Ms Riazi delivered a riveting and thought-provoking address as evinced by the highly focused Q&A session that followed.

Plenary Panel

Immediately after this on the program was the Women & Technology Leadership panel with Ms. Riazi, Holly Ross (executive director of the Drupal Association) and Angie 'webchick' Byron (co-maintainer of Drupal 7 and DA board member) which in addition to the on-site audience was watched by quite a high number of people around the world, to go by the real time feedback on social media.




Drupal ambassadors from 17 Countries Around the World

Out of all the leading source development projects Drupal stands out as being a global phenomenon bringing together code and community in the web's leading multilingual web framework. Drupal is also a diverse community itself with leads for various initiatives spread out across the globe. NYC Camp is one of the half-dozen or so annual events bringing them together to collaborate in real time. This year 17 countries were represented by over 1000 registered attendees. Hosting on campus brings to the UN unrivaled access to not just some of the most knowledgeable Drupalists in the world, but also some of the most innovative thinkers in the area of open source technology and planning.

Training hundreds of future technologists

NYC Camp Training Day has the effect of creating or improving the skills of hundreds of information workers though teaching technologies such as Drupal, Symfony, version control by some of the most in-demand Drupal trainers, in some cases flying in just for the day. All trainings were filled to maximum capacity, with extensive wait lists, indicating the tremendous hunger for this kind of Drupal / full stack training.



The next generation of technology leaders

While many of the problems facing the world today are of immediate urgency, some of them won't be solved until the next generation– and then only if we give them a chance. Drupal NG is directed at providing the structure and resources these future young visionaries need to build innovative solutions to the problems they see through a different set of eyes. As young people quickly taking to new technology, they often take to it more quickly than adults working in the same area. They're just learning new skills to put to work, by getting started at such an early age they're really the tech visionaries of tomorrow

UN Training Intensive

In addition to the general training tracks, an intensive training just for UN staff was arranged featuring sessions by the top minds in for each topic. So, Drupal's top multilingual contributor Gábor Hojtsy presented on internationalisation in Drupal 8, and Drupal security team lead Michael Hess lead a session just for those UN staff responsible for this area.

Higher Education Summit

The Annual Higher Education summit again brought leaders from the most recognized institutions of higher learning in the North East for a day of discussions of how they are using Drupal and related technologies, in order to compare best practices and solve common problems together.

This year is was also the first summit to resolve to occur more frequently, with follow up events planned for July (at Yale) and October (back in New York.)

The Fall event in particular might be well organized to feature the Unite Ideas platform.

Security Summit

(not recorded)

To get an idea of the value of the information disclosed at the summit: it was, by design, not recorded. Attendees to this are privy to an understanding of Drupal security practices that are not for public distribution.

The security summit had a very special guest keynote by Emmanuel Goldstein.

Multilingual Partnernership

TNYC Camp presents the unique opportunity for UN to work directly with Drupal community to develop multilingual solutions and to insure that the evolving needs of the United Nations continue to be met by the world's leading open source multilingual framework. Such a partnership between the UN and Drupal could conceivably chart the course for the next generation of international service delivery platforms on the World Wide Web, while forging new paths in Mobile Localization.

Coding for a Cause

This year at NYC Camp an idea that the guest CIO happened to mention in her Saturday keynote caught the attention of developers in attendance, and a working prototype was completely by the day after the camp ended. This is an idea she has been trying to get off the ground for years, and she got more out of coming to NYC Camp than years of chasing down other avenues. Acting on a suggestion from keynote speaker Robin Chase only the day before, the Coding for a Cause" sprint on the last day of NYC Camp started building a working prototype for DOable, the climate change solutions project heretofore only imagined by her.



Bringing New Ways of Thinking to Diverse Communities

Keynotes bringing mindshift / paradigm shift to hundreds of attendees: (language about changing the way people think about problems) with the hyper-connectedness of social media sending nuggets of wisdom on a viral virtual path around the world, changing minds and in so doing, creating the greater potential to change lives.

Such insights are brought to what is arguably the most successful open source project and most diverse open source community on the planet.



High Caliber Speakers

The success of the Drupal project worldwide, together in partnership with the UN's hosting of the well-established history of community organized annual events in New York City engender the possibility of "taking things to the next level" by attracting thinkers and visionaries who otherwise might have no opportunity to visit the UN. The success of series exemplified by TED Talks indicates the considerable demand for these kinds of visionary talks, and NYC Camp provides just such an opportunity to bring these deeply insightful and potentially world changing talks to the UN. Inspiring Developers
to do their Best Work

NYC Camp has a reputation for bringing out the best in everyone and this year was no exception, bringing together the top contributors to media handling in Drupal, and prompting a media team lead to say:

NYC Camp

Engendered a high degree of awareness of the UN's mission, not as an abstract concept, but as a lived experience.




Finally, and perhaps most crucially, inviting the Drupal development community to the UN to directly see the United Nations campus in person and all the people working together to fulfill its mission creates an immersive lived experience that transcends abstract knowledge of the UN's mission.

As one attendee put it:

"I've always known about the UN, of course, and thought I understood their mission. But it wasn't until I spent a couple days actually there that it really resonated with me. I felt as if NYC Camp somehow changed me, and that I came away with a new appreciation for the United Nations that I've never gotten from the outside."
